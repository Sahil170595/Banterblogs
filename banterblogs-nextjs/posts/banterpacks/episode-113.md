### Why It Matters
**The Humility of the Creator: Even Gods Can't Spell.**

Three minutes later. Fixed a typo in the README. "Inteligence" -> "Intelligence".

It's ironic. We are building an Artificial Intelligence, and we can't spell "Intelligence." It's like building a library and misspelling "Books" on the front door.

**Strategic Significance**: **Professionalism**. Typos make you look amateur. Even if the code is perfect, a typo in the README plants a seed of doubt. "If they can't spell, can they code?" "If they missed this, did they miss a buffer overflow?" "If they can't handle English, can they handle Python?" Perception is reality.

**Cultural Impact**: **Humility**. It reminds us that we are human. We make mistakes. We are not the machines we build. The machine would never make a typo (unless we trained it to). The machine is precise. We are messy. We are biological chaos engines trying to build silicon order.

**Foundation Value**: **Accuracy**. Details matter. Excellence is in the details. A typo is a broken window. It invites more carelessness. If you tolerate typos, you tolerate bugs.
